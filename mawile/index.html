background

I2C is a lovely hardware interface that allows multiple devices to communicate over the same data bus. By taking appropriate electrical design considerations, one can reach several devices. However, the Raspberry Pi 3 only provides two separate potential I2C connectors. This will not do. We must make more ports.

From specs on Pis, GPIO pins typically have ~5pF of input capacitance and are connected to internal ~65kΩ (worst-case) pull-up resistors. By limiting additional bus capacitances and operating in Standard-mode, rearranging Equation 1 (Section 7.1 of this) gives us Cb_max of ~18pF. By using low-capacitance connectors between the Pi, the companion board, and the slave devices, I predict that we will be able to communicate without errors with three more devices (~5pF each, parallel). The mawile project aims to find out.

break it down

On a Pi 3, pins 1-6 designate every connection (and more) needed to communicate over I2C. The mawile board extends SCL, SDA, VDD (+3.3V), and GND (0V) to four additional I2C ports.
